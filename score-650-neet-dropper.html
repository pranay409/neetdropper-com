How to Score 650+ in NEET as a Dropper — Exact Subject Strategy

📅 June 2026 · ⏱ 9 min read

Crossing 650 in NEET as a dropper requires a fundamentally different approach than a first attempt. You already know the pattern. You know your weak spots. The strategy is about exploiting what you know and systematically eliminating what hurt you last time. Here's the exact breakdown by subject.

Biology: 330+ out of 360 is the Target

Biology is 360 of 720 marks — 50% of NEET. For droppers targeting 650+, you need 330+ from Biology. This is not optional. The good news: Biology is almost entirely NCERT-based. No calculations, no derivations — just thorough NCERT reading and sharp memory.

High-priority chapters

  • Human Physiology (Class 11, Chapters 17-22) — reliably 15-20 questions per year
  • Genetics and Evolution (Class 12, Chapters 5-7) — 10-12 questions per year
  • Reproduction (Class 12, Chapters 1-4) — 10-12 questions per year
  • Plant Physiology (Class 11, Chapters 11-15) — 8-10 questions per year
  • Cell Biology (Class 11, Chapters 8-10) — 6-8 questions per year

Read NCERT Biology line by line — not chapter summaries, not handwritten notes from YouTube. The actual NCERT text. Questions that come directly from a specific sentence in NCERT are common. Any line you skip is a potential wrong answer.

⚠️ Biology Dropper Trap

Many droppers think they know Biology because they studied it last year. This overconfidence leads to shallow revision. Read NCERT completely, even chapters that feel familiar. A significant number of marks are lost in chapters students assumed they knew.

Physics: Target 140-150 out of 180

Physics is where most droppers have significant room to improve. The 2025 NEET paper showed that conceptually harder Physics questions are becoming more common. Pure formula memorisation isn't enough — conceptual clarity with numericals is the minimum.

High-priority chapters for droppers

  • Mechanics (Class 11: Chapters 3-8) — this is 25-30% of Physics marks
  • Electrostatics and Current Electricity (Class 12: Chapters 1-3) — reliably 15+ questions
  • Optics (Class 12: Chapters 9-10) — 8-10 questions every year
  • Modern Physics (Class 12: Chapter 12-13) — 8-10 questions
  • Thermodynamics (Class 11: Chapter 12) — 4-6 questions, very predictable

Chemistry: Target 165-175 out of 180

Chemistry is the most segmented subject — Organic, Inorganic, and Physical have very different approaches. For a 650+ dropper, you need all three sections performing well. You cannot ignore Inorganic (named reactions, properties) as many droppers do.

Organic Chemistry priorities

  • GOC (General Organic Chemistry) — foundational for all other chapters
  • Aldehydes, Ketones, Carboxylic Acids (Class 12, Chapter 12) — 5-6 questions
  • Amines, Biomolecules, Polymers — 4-5 questions combined

Inorganic priorities

  • P-Block Elements (Class 12, Chapter 7) — 6-8 questions per year
  • D and F Block, Coordination Compounds (Chapter 8-9) — 5-6 questions

Physical Chemistry priorities

  • Electrochemistry, Chemical Kinetics — 4-5 questions each, mostly numericals
  • Solutions, Equilibrium — 3-4 questions each

The Mock Test Strategy That Gets You to 650

After every mock test: mark every wrong answer with one of three labels — (1) Careless mistake, (2) Concept gap, (3) Never seen this. Careless mistakes need exam discipline practice. Concept gaps need targeted revision of that specific chapter. "Never seen" questions need chapter reading.

Within 3-4 months of consistent mock analysis, most droppers see their "concept gap" category shrink dramatically. The careless mistake category usually stays constant until you practice deliberate slowdown in question reading.
