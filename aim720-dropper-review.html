What's Actually Included in AIM720

  • 2-way live classes — not recorded lectures. Real-time interaction with faculty.
  • Personal mentor — a dedicated mentor who tracks your individual progress, reviews mock scores, and is reachable between sessions.
  • Study material — NCERT-aligned notes for Biology, Physics, and Chemistry.

The Personal Mentor — Why It Matters for Droppers

This is the most important differentiator. Every AIM720 student has a personal mentor — not a shared helpdesk, not a community TA, but an actual person who knows your weak chapters, tracks whether your mock scores are improving, and adapts your plan accordingly.

For dropper students, this is critical. Most drop years fail not because of lack of effort but because of drift — students study hard in the wrong areas or stop noticing they're slipping until February. A mentor who reviews your mock data weekly catches that drift in week two, not month four.

💡 The Dropper Difference

Padhle's AIM720 is the only major NEET coaching at this price point where a real person actively tracks your progress and adapts your plan. Every other option at ₹30,000 or less gives you content, not personal coaching.

The 2-Way Live Classes

Most "live" classes in ed-tech are live in name only — the teacher broadcasts, students watch. AIM720's 2-way format means students can ask questions in real time, get answers during the class, and flag confusion before it becomes a concept gap. For subjects like Physics and Organic Chemistry where doubt resolution speed matters enormously, this is a meaningful advantage over recorded lectures.

Is ₹30,000 Worth It?

Compare to the alternatives: Aakash offline for droppers costs ₹80,000–₹1.2 lakh with large batches and no personal mentor. Allen DLP costs ₹35,000–₹60,000 with no personal tracking. AIM720 at ₹30,000 provides more individualised support than both at a lower price.

The real question isn't "is ₹30,000 a lot" — it's "what is the cost of another failed attempt?" If another drop year means another year of family pressure, opportunity cost, and emotional weight, then the ₹30,000 investment that maximises this year's chances is clearly worth it.
